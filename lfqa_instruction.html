Instructions: Understanding the structure of answer for complex queries

Thank you for participating in this task! We would like to better understand what composes an answer to complex queries such as “Why do birds sing in the morning?”. You will be presented with a question, originally posted on Reddit forum, and an answer paragraph provided by another user. Your job is to provide a label for each sentence in the answer paragraph, deciding the role of this sentence in the paragraph.  


You have seven coarse categories of sentence roles (Response to the question, High level description of the answer provided, Background/Auxiliary information, Answer to the question, Providing an example, Elaboration of the answer, Others), which will be explained in detail below.


Example

Here’s an example annotation:

Question: Why do birds chirp more in the morning?


No. Answer Sentence Role
1 Birds will begin to chirp in the hours before dawn, this is known as the dawn chorus and can begin in earnest as early as 4 am (you might have a few keeners in your area). Response to the question
2 There are a couple of reasons why ornithologists think birds tend to sing most in the early hours... High level description of the answer provided
3 * First, the first to chirp is beating out the competition and is less likely to be drowned out by the completion later in the morning. Answer to the question
4 * Second, the air before dawn is usually still and thus much better at conducting sound. Answer to the question
5 More recent studies have shown that early-morning songs don't travel farther, but can be much clearer than later-in-the-day songs. Elaboration of the answer
6 A *'clear'* advantage for those early-birds. Elaboration of the answer
7 * Third, there isn't much to do in the early morning but sing since light levels are likely to low to forage Answer to the question

FAQ

Q: How can I distinguish between “Answer to the question” vs. “Background/Auxiliary Information” v.s. “Elaboration of the answer”?

A:
  • “Answer to the question” are the minimal set of sentences that can serve as an answer alone to the question provided. In other words, the removal of any sentence labeled with this role will result in an incomplete answer.
  • “Background/Auxiliary Information” provides information that might not be required to answer the question, but are either background information for some of the terms referred to in the answer or auxiliary information that are not asked but related to what is discussed in the answer. In the example below, sentence (1) - (4) talks about quantum mechnics that is related to “multi-verse” (Background information).
  • “Elaboration of the answer”contains further elaboration or explanation of the answers. In the example below, sentence (6) further explains sentence(5) and hence fall into this category.
Question: Please explain me how the string theory suggests the theoretical existence of the multiverse?


No. Answer Sentence Role
1 It's not so much string theory as it's about interpretations of how quantum mechanics works. Response to the question
2 In QM the world we see is fundamentally based upon chance; the waveform determines the chance the particle can be found at that state, but once we've measured the position the whole waveform 'collapses' and the particle 'is' in that specific state. Background / Auxiliary information
3 This places an observer in a weird spot where observing what happens fundamentally changes the outcome. Background / Auxiliary information
4 This goes against our intuition and also slightly against what scientists would want, we'd like to be neutral observers an not interfere with our experiments. Background / Auxiliary information
5 The multi-verse comes in here and basically says: the observer is not anything special, everything happens, the particle is in every possible state, but in different universes. Answer to the question
6 Which will mean that measuring simply tells you in 'what universe' you are in. Elaboration of the answer
7 Right now, however, there is no way to prove or disprove this theory, and I don't think anyone came up with even a theoretical way to test this. High level information of the answer
Q: What if a sentence serves two or more roles?

A: It is possible that one sentence serves more than one role. If that is the case, please select the most appropriate one for the sentence. For example: Question: Why is it better to use cloning software instead of just copying and pasting the entire drive?

No. Answer Sentence Role
1 In addition to what others have said, clone software can utilize block by block copying which can be much faster than copying than doing it file by file. Response to the question

Explanation: This sentence contains (1) response to other answers (“Others”): “in addition to what others have said” as well as (2) “Answer to the question”: “clone software can utilize block by block copying which can be much faster than copying than doing it file by file.” However since the second part is the main focus of the sentence, it should be labeled as the role “Answer to the question”.
